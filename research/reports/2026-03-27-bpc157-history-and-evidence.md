# BPC-157: History and Evidence Snapshot

Date: 2026-03-27

## Bottom line

BPC-157 has a long preclinical literature and a short human literature. The published record suggests genuine scientific interest since the 1990s, but it does not yet support strong claims of proven clinical benefit in humans.

## History in brief

The indexed literature reaches back to the 1990s, with early wound-healing papers describing BPC-157 as a gastric pentadecapeptide with broad protective effects in experimental models. A large share of the older literature is connected to the same research network in Croatia, especially around Zagreb.

By 2007, papers were already referring to BPC-157 as having been used in inflammatory bowel disease trials under identifiers such as PL-10, PLD-116, and PL14736. That point matters historically, but the modern public record still has a thin set of accessible, published human trial results.

## What counts as a real study here

For this project, real studies include:

- indexed journal articles in PubMed
- registered human trial records on ClinicalTrials.gov
- official FDA or USADA/WADA guidance when it changes the real-world interpretation of claims

The library deliberately separates these from podcasts, vendor pages, wellness-clinic marketing, and secondhand social media summaries.

## The evidence stack

### 1. Preclinical evidence is abundant

There are many rodent and cell-based studies on:

- wound healing
- tendon and ligament repair
- gastrointestinal injury models
- angiogenesis and nitric oxide signaling

Representative examples include the 1997 healing paper, the 2010 rat ligament paper, and the 2011 tendon fibroblast/mechanistic paper. These studies are useful for plausibility, but they are not substitutes for controlled human trials.

### 2. Human evidence is still sparse

The clearest human-facing items identified in this pass are:

- a registered phase I trial record in healthy volunteers (`NCT02637284`) with unknown status and no posted results
- a small 2021 retrospective knee-pain study with 17 charts and non-standardized follow-up
- a 2025 two-person intravenous safety pilot reporting no adverse effects in those two participants

This is too little to justify confident clinical claims.

### 3. Recent reviews still point to the same gap

The 2025 HSS Journal systematic review is the cleanest recent synthesis for musculoskeletal use. It reported 36 included studies from 1993 to 2024:

- 35 preclinical studies
- 1 clinical study

That ratio captures the core state of the evidence base.

## Public-communication stance

The current public site and internal notes should describe BPC-157 this way:

- historically interesting
- preclinically active in many models
- weakly supported in humans
- not approved for routine clinical use
- risky to overstate because regulatory and anti-doping bodies still treat it as an unapproved substance
